The Non-Technical Guide to APIs

You don't need to write code. You need to sound fluent in outcomes.

Your primary goal is not to write code, configure servers, or debug software. Your job is to uncover business bottlenecks and present meCash as the ultimate financial bridge.

What is an API?

API stands for Application Programming Interface — a software messenger that allows two completely different computer systems to talk to each other, share data, and execute tasks automatically, without human intervention.

Wallet API

Create, hold, and manage digital money accounts (wallets) inside any platform — like giving your software its own internal banking ledger.

Solves

Without this, companies maintain error-prone Excel sheets to track which user owns what balance. The Wallet API automates the entire ledger.

FintechsDigital BanksInvestment AppsCrypto ExchangesSuper AppsGig Platforms

  • Automated segregation of customer vs. corporate funds

FX & Currency

Quote API

Live guaranteed foreign exchange conversion rates that lock in the exact wholesale price for a set window of time.

Solves

Currency markets fluctuate every second. Without a rate lock, merchants lose profit margins to exchange rate swings between quoting and receiving payment.

Disbursement

An automated money-sending engine that instantly disburses individual payments to external bank accounts or mobile wallets globally.

Solves

Manual payout processes require finance staff to log into bank portals, enter account numbers one-by-one, and authorize transfers individually — agonizingly slow and error-prone.

💡 Analogy: Like an automated ATM built inside your office — type a name, cash materializes in their account.

Mass Disbursement

Bulk Payout API

An industrial-strength disbursement tool — dispatches thousands of individual payments simultaneously with a single batch command.

Solves

Sending 50,000 individual API calls sequentially could overload systems and take hours. Bulk Payout bundles them into one massively optimized batch.

Best For

💡 Analogy: Like a corporate mailroom printing press — not one letter, but 100,000 simultaneously on an assembly line.

Collection & Reconciliation

Virtual Accounts API

Unique, programmable digital bank account numbers — real-looking accounts that route funds instantly into a master wallet with full payer identification.

Static vs. Dynamic

Static VA: Permanent account assigned forever to ONE customer (like a private P.O. Box). Dynamic VA: Temporary, self-destructing account tied to ONE shopping cart with a timer (like a coat-check ticket).

Best For
B2B WholesaleReal EstateSchoolsLendersUtilitiesCrypto
Key Benefits
  • 100% automated reconciliation — no manual matching ever
  • Zero chargebacks (bank transfers are irreversible)
  • Displays your company's brand name in banking apps
  • Instantly rejects or flags wrong-amount transfers
Mobile-First Collection

Directly pull or receive funds from customer mobile wallets (M-Pesa, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, Orange Money) across Africa.

Solves

✅ Webhooks: The shop texts you the exact second your driver pulls in.

Section 06

Customer Discovery Questions

Amateurs pitch products. Masters ask brilliant questions and let customers realize their own operations are broken.

The Discovery Framework: Uncover Current Flow → Identify the Pain / Cost → Expose the Scalability Trap

Category A

Inflow & Collection — Finding the "Getting Paid" Friction

1"Walk me through the exact journey of a customer paying you today — from the moment they decide to buy to the moment your bank confirms the cash."▼
Why ask this: Forces them to map out their own friction. You are looking for manual verification steps, human interventions, and delays in the process.
2"What percentage of your customers currently drop out or abandon their purchase at your payment checkout screen?"▼
Why ask this: Exposes poor conversion rates caused by relying solely on credit card gateways in mobile-first markets. Leads to Mobile Money Collection pitch.
3"When customers pay you via standard bank transfers, how do your staff actually figure out which customer sent which deposit?"▼
Why ask this: Instantly uncovers the massive manual reconciliation nightmare that leads directly to pitching Virtual Accounts.
4"How frequently do your customers make typos in their payment reference numbers, and how much customer service time is wasted hunting down those 'lost' payments?"▼
Why ask this: Quantifies the human cost of unreferenced payments — makes the Virtual Accounts value proposition impossible to ignore.
5"Are you currently turning away mass-market consumers because they want to pay with M-Pesa, MTN MoMo, or Orange Money, but you only accept Visa/Mastercard?"▼
Why ask this: Exposes a massive blind spot in their Total Addressable Market (TAM). In markets like Ghana and Uganda, card penetration is under 15%.
6"What is your average ticket size, and how much of your profit margin is currently being eaten alive by standard percentage-based credit card processing fees?"▼
Why ask this: Positions Mobile Money Collection or Bank Transfers as a massive cost-reduction play — fixed low fees vs. high card percentages.
Category B

Outflow & Disbursements — Finding the "Paying Out" Friction

7"When you need to pay out 500 vendors, contractors, or employees, what is the exact step-by-step process your finance manager goes through?"▼
Why ask this: Uncovers manual bank portal usage, token passing, and extreme administrative slowness. Leads directly to Bulk Payout API pitch.
8"How many hours or days does your team spend executing your monthly payroll or vendor settlement runs?"▼
Why ask this: Translates administrative headache directly into wasted financial expense — salaries paid to staff just to type numbers into a bank portal.
9"What happens when your staff accidentally types the wrong account number during a payout run? How long does it take to recover those trapped funds?"▼
Why ask this: Highlights the critical lack of automated account pre-validation — and the dangerous manual errors it enables.
10"If a gig worker or seller on your platform demands to withdraw their earnings at 11:00 PM on a Saturday night, can your current system settle them instantly?"▼
Why ask this: Uncovers the inability to operate a 24/7 real-time business model. Leads to Payout API. Connects to driver churn narrative.
11"How do you handle mass payout failures when local bank networks experience temporary downtime or timeouts?"▼
Why ask this: Exposes their lack of automated retry logic and multi-rail fallback redundancy. Positions meCash's Intelligent Multi-Rail Routing.
12"Are your contractors constantly complaining or churning because your payment cycles take one to two weeks to reach their accounts?"▼
Why ask this: Connects slow disbursement rails directly to high operational churn — a P&L impact the CEO and COO care deeply about.

✓ What Masters Do

Always ask to involve Finance early. During discovery, explicitly ask: "When your software captures these payments, how does your finance team actually pull the audit trails to drop into your ERP software?" Pitch the Transaction API alongside collection tools to prove you solve the bookkeeping headache too.

Mistake 6

Bashing Competitors Instead of Educating

❌ What Amateurs Do

The prospect says "We are currently using Stripe." The amateur rep says: "Stripe is terrible in Africa! They are super expensive and fail all the time! You should fire them immediately!" — This makes you look insecure, arrogant, and potentially insults someone who personally chose Stripe.

✓ What Masters Do

Use the "Compliment & Elevate" technique. "Stripe is a world-class platform — their developer documentation is incredible. However, because their architecture was built for Western credit card networks, in emerging markets they are forced to route through third-party middlemen and double-convert currencies, eating your margins. We built natively on local banking clearinghouses."
